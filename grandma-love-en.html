Grandma's 10 Ways
to Say I Love You

Love doesn't always need words 👵❤️

Ages 4–8 · 13 Pages · Interactive

🏡 For every grandma in the world

Grandma never said "I love you" out loud.

She was from a different time, a different place.
Where people said love differently.

But she said it — every single day.
In ten beautiful ways. 💛

Way #1 🍜

She cooked your favourite food.

She woke up before sunrise.
She stirred the pot for hours.
She didn't say "I love you."

She placed the bowl in front of you and said:
"Eat. Eat more. You're too thin."

That was love. 🍜

Way #2 🤗

She hugged you without reason.

Sometimes, out of nowhere —
she'd stop what she was doing.
Walk over.
And just hold you tight for a minute.

No reason. No words.
Just her arms, and the smell of her cardigan, and warmth.

That was love. 🤗

Way #3 📖

She told you stories at bedtime.

Long after bedtime,
her voice filled the dark room.

Stories of dragons and faraway kingdoms.
Stories of when she was young.
Stories you'd heard a hundred times — and still wanted again.

That was love. 📖

Way #4 🧵

She fixed everything you broke.

Toys with broken arms. Shirts with missing buttons.
Holes in your favourite socks.

Grandma always had a needle and thread ready.
She'd squint and thread and stitch — and make it whole again.

She didn't say "I love you."
She handed it back, good as new.
That was love. 🧵

Way #10 👀

She looked at you like you were the whole world.

Out of all ten ways,
this one was the most powerful.

When she looked at you —
really looked, with her whole heart —

You felt it.
Like you were the only person in the whole world.
Like you mattered more than anything. 💗

I love you, Grandma. 👵❤️
Love doesn't always come in words. 💛 Sometimes it comes in a bowl of soup, 🍜 a tight hug at the door, 🤗 or a look that says everything. ❤️ 💛 THE END 💛
